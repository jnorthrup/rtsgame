# Game Mechanics Feedback Request

## Introduction

We are in the initial stages of developing a new game and would love your feedback on the core mechanics. Please review the concepts below and share your thoughts, suggestions, and any concerns you might have. Your input will be invaluable in shaping the game's direction.

## 1.A. Matter-to-Energy & Resources

This section outlines the proposed system for resource gathering and energy production.

**Key Concepts:**

*   **Regolith:** A common, undifferentiated resource.
*   **Elemental Veins:** Concentrated deposits of specific elements, rarer than Regolith.
*   **Wattage Types:** Different forms of energy (e.g., thermal, kinetic, electrical) that may have unique applications or efficiencies.
*   **Conversion Processes:** Mechanisms for transforming matter (Regolith, Elemental Veins) into energy (Wattage types) and potentially into other usable resources.

**Feedback Prompts:**

*   Does this resource and energy system sound engaging?
*   Are there any obvious exploits or imbalances you foresee?
*   What are your thoughts on different Wattage types? Is this an interesting complexity, or an unnecessary one?
*   Do you have suggestions for specific conversion processes or technologies?

## 1.B. Volumetric Terrain Change

This section describes how players can alter the game world's terrain.

**Key Concepts:**

*   **Direct Costs:** Altering terrain will consume resources (likely energy and/or specific materials).
*   **Strategic Implications:** Terrain modification can be used for defense (e.g., creating barriers, funnels), offense (e.g., undermining structures, creating new attack paths), or resource access.
*   **Mike Mulligan-style Alterations:** Implying significant, potentially large-scale and impactful terrain changes, rather than minor tweaks.

**Feedback Prompts:**

*   How appealing is the idea of large-scale, dynamic terrain alteration?
*   What are your initial thoughts on the strategic possibilities this offers?
*   Are there any concerns about performance or complexity with such a system?
*   Can you think of any interesting examples of how this could be used in gameplay?

## 1.C. Warfare Components & Mass

This section touches on the elements of combat and unit construction.

**Key Concepts:**

*   **Magnetics:** Likely for railguns, magnetic armor, containment fields, etc.
*   **Projectiles:** Various types of ammunition, possibly with different material properties affecting performance.
*   **Alloys:** Combining elements to create materials with specific combat properties (e.g., enhanced durability, reduced weight).
*   **Metamaterials:** Advanced materials with unique, potentially physics-bending properties for high-tier components.
*   **Mass:** The weight and density of components will likely be a critical factor in unit design, affecting speed, maneuverability, and energy consumption.

**Feedback Prompts:**

*   Does this approach to warfare components seem deep and interesting?
*   What are your thoughts on the interplay between different component types (Magnetics, Projectiles, etc.)?
*   How important should 'Mass' be as a design constraint?
*   Are there other warfare concepts or component types you think would fit well?

## 1.D. Generative AI Integration

This section explores how AI could be integrated into the gameplay loop.

**Key Concepts:**

*   **Component Design:** AI could assist players in designing new components, potentially generating novel designs based on player-defined parameters or discovered technologies.
*   **Process Optimization:** AI could help optimize resource conversion, manufacturing chains, or energy grids.
*   **Strategic Advice:** AI could offer players strategic suggestions, analyze enemy threats, or identify opportunities.

**Feedback Prompts:**

*   What is your initial reaction to integrating Generative AI in these ways?
*   Which of these AI applications (Component Design, Process Optimization, Strategic Advice) excites you the most?
*   Do you have any concerns about AI potentially "playing the game" for the player or reducing player agency?
*   Are there other ways you could see Generative AI enhancing the game experience?

## General Feedback

Please also feel free to provide any general feedback or thoughts you have on these initial concepts.

Thank you for your time and input!
